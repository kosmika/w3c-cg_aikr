The AI landscape is dense, chaotic, and evolving exponentially. In this context, terms, concepts, and initiatives overlap and are frequently ambiguous, making it more difficult to build the shared understanding necessary to achieve interoperability and trust in agentic systems.The AI Knowledge Representation Community Group (AI KR CG) seeks to reduce the gaps in shared concepts and semantics. It monitors the evolution of AI across multiple dimensions — identifying, mapping, and analysing the primitives emerging from research and related standardization efforts. Using canonical knowledge-representation techniques, the AI KR CG builds shared, explicit representations — categories, taxonomies, and vocabularies — that support alignment, consistency, and model integrity across disparate initiatives, giving stakeholders common terms of reference for a fast-moving field.Through work on taxonomies, vocabularies, a standards inventory, gap analysis, and more, the Community Group hopes to help individuals and organisations stay up to date with developments in the field and achieve a deeper understanding of the ongoing evolution of the agentic-AI landscape

This repository holds shared notes and memos aimed at informing AI KR CG participants about the state of the art, through the representation and definition of key terms and concepts in the fast-moving agentic-AI landscape.

The content of these files is open for discussion, evaluation, and refinement. Because the space is emergent and the work is iterative, some of the older files may contain duplicate, missing, or redundant information that has since been merged into the newer files below. Nothing here is a W3C Standard or on the W3C Standards Track.

Participants are welcome to contact the chair, discuss via the public mailing list, or suggest how to develop these resources further.

Start here — the core resources

Three linked working drafts form the current spine of the group’s agentic-AI work. They share one structure — eight knowledge domains — and are the documents most in need of your review and contribution. All are working drafts, provisional and open for review.

  • Agentic AI Knowledge Domains — Taxonomy For Review
    W3C AI KR CG  •  Working draft
    The top-level category map: eight knowledge domains under which the vocabulary and the standards inventory are organised. Each term links to a current source; entries are provisional.
  • Agentic AI Vocabulary For Review
    W3C AI KR CG  •  Working draft
    A controlled vocabulary of ~150 agentic-AI terms with proposed definitions, sources, and SKOS-style relations, organised by the eight taxonomy categories, plus an acronym quick-reference.
  • Agentic AI Standards Inventory & Gap Analysis For Review
    W3C AI KR CG  •  Working draft
    An inventory of current agentic-AI standards and efforts with a per-domain coverage/gap assessment and recommended work items, organised by the eight taxonomy categories.

Repository contents — reference material

Earlier and supporting notes. Several of these fed into the three core resources above; some information they contain has since been merged or superseded. Retained for reference and history.

  • Summary & Repository Map For Review
    A ReSpec overview of the group’s purpose and how the documents in this repository fit together.
  • Repository File Map (interactive) For Review
    An interactive graph of the repository documents and their links to neighbouring W3C and IETF standards.
  • Standards Landscape Map (interactive) For Review
    An interactive board placing each effort by framework layer (representation → trust) and openness.
  • Agentic AI Standards Inventory (earlier) Superseded
    The earlier standards inventory; largely consolidated into the Standards Inventory & Gap Analysis in the core resources above.
  • Agent Ontology Alignment: IDL and HMAS CG-Draft
    A use-case alignment (not a new ontology) between the IDL Agent Ontology and the HMAS ontology stack.
  • Open Standard Vocabularies for E-commerce and the Agentic Interoperability Gap For Review
    The open e-commerce vocabulary stack and what it lacks for autonomous agents; input to the W3C/GS1 workshop.
  • Digital Forensics Model Card Specification CG-Draft
    A model-card framework for AI/ML systems used in digital and web forensics, with a JSON schema and reference implementation.
  • WebMCP Technical Notes Editor’s Draft
    Notes on the proposed WebMCP browser API and its relationship to W3C Web Machine Learning incubation work.
  • Voice AI Standards — ReSpec Draft Editor’s Draft
    Requirements and design principles for voice AI systems, with analysis of documented deployment failures.
  • Voice AI Standards — With New Types Editor’s Draft
    Extended version with new voice-AI interaction-type classifications; from the W3C Smart Voice Agents Workshop.
  • Guide to Solid Editor’s Draft
    A practical introduction to the W3C Solid protocol for content creators, AI agent developers, and users.
  • Conduit Feedback Editor’s Draft
    Feedback posted to the AI Agents Protocols CG (March 2026).
  • Guidelines for Writing Machine-Consumable Specifications Technical Note
    Guidance on authoring specifications readable and consumable by AI agents and automated tools.
  • Accessibility and Sustainability Principles for AI Code Assistants Technical Note
    Principles for accessibility and sustainability in AI code assistants.
  • Community Note: Code Quality Principles for AI-Generated Code Note
    Quality and accessibility principles for code generated by AI systems.
  • Inventory of Efforts Related to A2WF Note
    A survey of efforts intersecting the Agent-to-Web Framework (A2WF).
  • Understanding robots.txt — A Primer for the Age of AI Agents Primer
    An explainer on robots.txt and content-usage signals in the context of AI agents.
